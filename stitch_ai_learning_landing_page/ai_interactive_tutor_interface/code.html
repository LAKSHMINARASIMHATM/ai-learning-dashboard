Core Concepts

Quantum entanglement is a physical phenomenon that occurs when a pair or group of particles is generated, defined such that the quantum state of each particle cannot be described independently of the state of the others.

Even when separated by a large distance, a measurement of one particle's property immediately influences the possible results of the other particle's state.
